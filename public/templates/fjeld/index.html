The days

The programme is the absence of one.

Heat, food, silence, weather. Everything else is optional, and most of it is downhill.

Move the mouse, or scroll sideways
01

Heat

The sauna holds ninety degrees and four people, in that order of importance. One bucket, one ladle, one window the size of the view. Cold water is provided by Norway.

02

The table

Dinner is one seating and one menu, written at noon when the kitchen sees what the valley sent up. Cured char, mountain lamb, cloudberries when we are lucky. Wine list short and opinionated.

03

Silence

No spa menu, no activity board, no speaker in the ceiling. The lounge has a fire, a wall of maps and a policy: whoever falls asleep in the armchair owns it until dinner.

The journal

Notes from 987 metres.

Written by the keeper, on paper first, weather permitting. Typed up when the fog is too thick to work outside.

Dear guest. The lodge was my grandfather's, and for forty years it slept nine hikers a night on bunks that forgave nothing. We rebuilt it in 2021 with more glass and fewer opinions.

People ask what there is to do here. I keep a straight face and say: the weather. It changes every twenty minutes and it has never once repeated itself. Everything else we offer (the sauna, the table, the armchairs) is just a good seat for watching it work.

Come tired. Leave slowly.

Nils Fjærland, keeper

12 Feb 2026

The first proper dump

Sixty centimetres in a night. The plough driver arrived at six looking personally offended. Bre's skylight held a metre of blue light until noon, and the guests who were booked to leave on Tuesday are, as of this writing, still here. Nobody has complained, least of all the kitchen.

28 Oct 2025

What the fog takes

A guest asked for a refund on the view. Fair. The valley had been a white wall for two days. Then at four the fog dropped below the sauna window in one slow curtain, and the whole slope lit up amber, and the same guest asked if he could extend. The fog takes, the fog gives back doubled. We do not negotiate with it.

06 Jul 2025

A recipe for cured char

Salt, sugar, dill, juniper you crushed yourself. Forty-eight hours under a weight, sliced thin enough to read through. Serve to someone who claims they do not like fish and enjoy the silence that follows. The lake is twelve minutes down the west trail; the recipe is older than the lodge.
