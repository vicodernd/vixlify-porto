01 · The clay

Fired twice.
Glazed once.

We throw with a single stoneware body, iron-rich and dug in Shigaraki. It bisque-fires low, takes one coat of glaze by hand, then goes to the kiln for a slow reduction. No two pieces leave the same. The marks of the wheel stay.

Three hands between the clay and your table.

STEP 01
You shape it

Turn the vessel, settle on a form, a glaze and a size. Your spec is written straight onto the order card.

STEP 02
We throw it

The piece is thrown on the wheel to your form, trimmed, then glazed once by hand. Roughly five to six weeks.

STEP 03
The kiln decides

A slow reduction firing brings the glaze up. We photograph the result and ship it wrapped in the studio's own paper.
